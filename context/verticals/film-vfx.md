## Use Cases

1. **Previsualization:** Directors generate 3D scenes to plan shots before production
2. **Concept exploration:** Art directors iterate on visual concepts rapidly
3. **Virtual set extension:** Generate backgrounds and environments for compositing
4. **Storyboarding in 3D:** Turn storyboard sketches into explorable environments
5. **Location scouting:** Generate variations of locations without physical travel

## Why It Matters for World Labs

- **High willingness to pay:** Film/VFX studios have large budgets for tools
- **Quality-first market:** These users value output quality over generation speed
- **Professional validation:** Film industry endorsement (SHoP, Preview.io) builds credibility across all verticals
- **Export pipeline fit:** SPZ and GLB exports integrate into existing VFX workflows

## Adoption Barriers

- **Existing tool investment:** Studios have deep investments in Maya, Houdini, Nuke, Unreal
- **Precision requirements:** Film needs exact control over lighting, camera, materials — generation is probabilistic
- **Collaboration workflows:** Film production is highly collaborative — World Labs is currently single-user
- **DCC round-tripping:** Even with in-product editing (Chisel and Marble Studio ship today, surfaced via "Create & edit" in the omnibox), generated environments still need clean hand-off into existing DCC tools (Maya, Houdini, Nuke) for final shot work
