# Gaming & Immersive Media Vertical

> All information sourced from publicly available data. Tagged [PUBLIC].

## Overview

Turn 2D content into explorable 3D worlds for games, interactive entertainment, and immersive experiences. This is World Labs' most visible vertical — Christoph Lassner's "Content 3.0" vision.

## Use Cases

1. **Game environment generation:** Create diverse game levels and environments from text/image prompts at scale
2. **Film previsualization:** Directors explore scenes before building sets or shooting
3. **Interactive storytelling:** Lassner's "Content 3.0" — viewers become participants in AI-generated narratives
4. **Virtual production:** Generate 3D backdrops for film/TV production
5. **VR/AR content:** Generate immersive environments for headset experiences

## Why It Matters for World Labs

- **Highest engagement potential:** Gaming/entertainment users spend the most time in generated worlds
- **Strongest consumer appeal:** "Generate a world and explore it" is inherently fun and viral
- **Lassner's domain expertise:** His Epic Games background gives World Labs credibility here
- **Content 3.0 narrative:** This is the most compelling vision story for fundraising and press

## Adoption Barriers

- **Quality expectations are extreme:** Gamers compare against AAA game engines (Unreal, Unity)
- **Interactivity gap:** Generated worlds are static — no physics, NPCs, or gameplay mechanics
- **Performance requirements:** Games need 60fps rendering, not 5-minute generation
- **Integration complexity:** Game engines have their own asset pipelines; World Labs assets need to fit

## Market Signals

- Google Genie 3 targets this vertical with real-time generation (but no persistence or export)
- Unity and Unreal are investing in AI-assisted content creation tools
- Indie game developers are the most likely early adopters (lower quality bar, more experimental)
